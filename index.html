Interest: to advance scalable computer vision and multimodal learning algorithms inspired by, but not limited to, the challenges of human brain & math.

Current role. I lead data-acquisition efforts at an electron microscopy facility at Harvard as part of a collaboration across Harvard, Princeton, Google, MIT, the Allen Institute, Cambridge University, and Johns Hopkins University. The project aims to reconstruct 10 mm³ of adult mouse cortex at nanometer resolution. I operate and optimize the imaging pipeline, develop high-throughput quality-control workflows, and perform downstream analysis. The pipeline combines a custom tissue mill with a 91-beam scanning electron microscope (Zeiss MultiSEM) for automated, record-speed imaging of large neural volumes.

About

short bio

Hi! I’m Fuming (Felix) Yang. I work at the interface of algorithms, neuroscience, and systems. Right now I help build a petavoxel‑scale connectomics pipeline and design brain‑inspired learning rules that translate into efficient ML.

Current role. I lead data‑acquisition efforts at an electron microscopy facility at Harvard as part of a collaboration across Harvard, Princeton, Google, MIT, the Allen Institute, Cambridge University, and Johns Hopkins University. The project aims to reconstruct 10 mm³ of adult mouse cortex at nanometer resolution. I operate and optimize the imaging pipeline, develop high‑throughput quality‑control workflows, and perform downstream analysis. The pipeline combines a custom tissue mill with a 91‑beam scanning electron microscope (Zeiss MultiSEM) for automated, record‑speed imaging of large neural volumes.
